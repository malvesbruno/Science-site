Under Sea

The ocean covers 71% of the Earth's surface. It also holds about 96.5% of all Earth's water – that's a volume of 320 million cubic miles! The globe would be 29% bigger if all the water were removed

The ocean is home to over 230,000 known species of fish, which is more than all the other types of animals on land combined

The ocean is also home to many other creatures, such as whales, dolphins, sea turtles, and jellyfish

The ocean plays a vital role in the Earth's climate, regulating the temperature and weather patterns. It also provides food and oxygen for us and other living things

The deepest point in the ocean is the Mariana Trench, which is over 36,000 feet deep. That's deeper than Mount Everest is tall!

The ocean is still a largely unexplored place, and there is much that we don't know about it. However, scientists are constantly learning new things about the ocean, and it is one of the most fascinating places on Earth.
